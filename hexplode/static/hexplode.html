The board comprises an array of hexagonal tiles, on which the players take turns to place counters. When the number of counters on a given tile equals the number of adjacent tiles the tile "hexplodes" and the contents of the tile are equally distributed to its neighbours changing the ownership of neighbouring counters over to the current player in the process. This process can often generate a chain reaction. The winner is the player that removes all of their opponents counters from the board.
